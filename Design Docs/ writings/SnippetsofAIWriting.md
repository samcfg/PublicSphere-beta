  The vision at full scope:

  1. Discourse that Accretes Rather Than Resets
  Current deliberation is Sisyphean - every forum thread, every comment section, every debate starts from scratch. Arguments made a
  thousand times, refuted a thousand times, made again. PublicSphere breaks this cycle. Arguments build on what came before. A
  well-structured contradiction to a claim becomes permanent context for anyone encountering that claim. Knowledge compounds instead
   of evaporating.

  2. Precision in Disagreement
  Most disputes dissolve into vague talking-past. "We just see it differently." But PublicSphere forces specificity: Where exactly
  do you diverge? Which source do you dispute? Which logical step? This doesn't eliminate disagreement - it clarifies it. You might
  discover you agree on 80% of the structure and differ on one empirical claim. Or one value assumption hidden in a connection's
  warrant.

  3. Distributed Expertise Converging
  Complex questions - climate policy, economic systems, medical interventions - require knowledge no single person possesses. But
  traditionally, synthesis is done by gatekeepers (journalists, academics, bureaucrats). PublicSphere enables peer synthesis -
  specialists contribute their domains, the graph shows how pieces fit, contradictions surface, and collective understanding emerges
   from transparent process rather than editorial authority.

  4. Trust Through Auditability
  In an age of information manipulation, the ability to trace reasoning to foundations is crucial. Not "trust this expert," but
  "follow the chain yourself." The graph becomes a trust mechanism - you can audit the path from claim to evidence, rate the logic,
  propose alternatives.

  Consider the cathedral builders of Chartres - masons who laid foundations they would never see crowned with spire. Each stone
  positioned for successors they would never meet, guided by geometric principles that would hold for centuries. PublicSphere
  aspires to this: arguments built in layers by many hands, where each contribution bears weight for those who climb higher.

  The medieval builders had their plumb lines and sacred geometry. We have logical necessity, empirical verification, the recursive
  structure of claim resting on warrant resting on evidence. Different tools, same instinct - to build something that stands because
   it is true in its joinery.

  Atavista - the backward glance that orients forward motion.

  The Enlightenment project promised: give reasons, show your work, submit to scrutiny. Kant's sapere aude - dare to know. But the
  tools betrayed the promise. Print could distribute, but not interconnect. Libraries preserved, but couldn't cross-reference at the
   speed of thought. Radio and television spoke to millions but silenced response.

  PublicSphere returns to that original aspiration with tools the philosophes couldn't dream of. Rousseau imagined the general will
  emerging from rational deliberation. He had no mechanism for it at scale. We might.

  This is atavistic in the true sense - reaching back to reclaim what was lost, what was only partially realized. The Socratic
  method, the dialectic, the symposium - these were always meant to build understanding through structured exchange. We're not
  inventing this. We're scaling ancestral wisdom with modern infrastructure.
